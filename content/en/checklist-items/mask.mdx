Police and ICE use [facial recognition against dissidents](https://www.kenklippenstein.com/p/ice-making-list-of-anyone-who-films).

Right-wing adversaries often photograph protesters to run reverse image searches and identify participants—some maintain databases like [Canary Mission](https://canarymission.org/) with activist profiles. Hiding some or all of your face makes this harder.

A KN95 mask can help you avoid facial recognition tools. It's not perfect (some tools only need your eyes), but it helps significantly.

**Other identifying features matter too.** A face covering alone isn't enough if other parts of you are easy to recognize. Adversaries often identify masked protesters by their distinctive tattoos, hair color or style, body shape, skin tone, and clothing, especially when several of these are combined. Consider covering visible tattoos with long sleeves, wearing a hat or hood, and rotating your protest clothing if you want to be harder to track across actions.

<Alert type="info">
  As with other items on this list, privacy and visibility can be in tension: some may want to minimize risk by covering their face and eyes, while others prefer showing their full face to build trust and convey emotions more directly.
</Alert>

Legal note: Some cities have passed anti-masking laws that make it illegal to wear a mask. You may want to check your local city ordinances.
