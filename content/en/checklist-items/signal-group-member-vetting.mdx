A large Signal group is only as secure as its vetting process. Once someone is in the group, they can read everything: past messages if disappearing messages aren't enabled, and all future messages regardless. Law enforcement uses infiltrators, and right-wing adversaries use them too. A single bad-faith member in a 200-person group can silently document everything.

The challenge is verifying someone without exposing them or others in the process. These methods give you a way to confirm identity without broadcasting real names to the whole group.

**Send a private message first.** If someone who has requested to join is already a Signal contact, message them directly to confirm they sent the request. This takes 30 seconds and rules out someone else using their account.

**Connect through a platform you already trust.** If you've interacted with someone on another platform (email list, other group chat, in-person meeting), reach out there to confirm the request is real.

**Use a brief voice or video call for new members.** For people you don't know at all, a short call confirms you're talking to a real person, not a forwarded join link.

**Ask other members to vouch via DM, not in the group.** If you don't know someone but think another member might, ask that member directly rather than posting in the group. Responses posted in the group can link Signal names to real names for everyone watching.

<HowTo title="How to see and manage join requests">
  Open the group chat > tap the group name > **Requests & Invites**

  You'll see pending requests here. You can approve or deny each one individually.

  You can also see who invited existing members. Useful if you're trying to trace how someone got into the group.
</HowTo>

**Check the group roster periodically.** Even after vetting, it's worth reviewing the member list every few months to make sure everyone there was intentionally added. If you see an account you don't recognize, send them a direct message before removing them.

<Alert type="warning">
  Never post someone's legal name, employer, neighborhood, or other identifying details in the group chat as part of a vetting conversation. If you need to confirm who someone is, do it in a direct message.
</Alert>

<Alert type="success">
  Write your vetting process into your group description or a pinned message so admins are aligned on how it works. A documented process is harder to shortcut under pressure.
</Alert>
