The default backups that WhatsApp creates to iCloud or Google Drive are NOT end-to-end encrypted. That means a court can compel Apple or Google to turn over your entire chat history. Even though your messages were sent securely, the backup defeats that protection by providing an easy way for your entire chat history to be revealed.

Disabling backups also protects you if a hacker breaks into your iCloud or Google account through phishing or a reused password.

<HowTo title="How to disable backups entirely">
  <MenuPath>*iPhone:* WhatsApp > Tap **You** in the bottom bar > **Chats** > **Chat Backup** > set **Auto Backup** to **Off**</MenuPath>

  <MenuPath>*Android:* WhatsApp > ⋮ > **Chats** > **Chat Backup** > set **Auto Backup** to **Off**</MenuPath>
  
  <Alert type="warning">
    **Tell your friends:** Even if your backups are locked down, people you chat with may be backing up unencrypted. Their backup includes your messages. Ask contacts to lock down their backups too. You might want to send them this guide.
  </Alert>
</HowTo>

If you absolutely need backups, see [Securely back up your WhatsApp chats](/whatsapp/#whatsapp-secure-backup) for how to do this safely.
