[Signal](https://signal.org/) messages are often sensitive and something we want to protect from government surveillance.

The [Signal backup feature](https://signal.org/blog/introducing-secure-backups/) is a great way to ensure your messages stay safe when you're headed into a situation where your phone might be confiscated or searched.

### Backup Signal data and Delete Signal

<HowTo title="How to backup Signal data and delete Signal">
  1. **Enable Signal backups**:  <MenuPath inline>Open Signal > Tap your profile picture > **Settings** > **Backups** > **Enable Backups**</MenuPath>
  2. **Save your recovery key**:  Save a copy of your recovery key in a secure location. We recommend using a [password manager like 1Password](/essentials/#password-manager). 
      * If you you want to be as safe as possible, you can send the recovery key to a trusted friend or family member who can read it back to you when you restore the backup later (so it's not stored digitally anywhere).
  3. **Confirm your recovery key**: Signal will prompt you to confirm your recovery key to prove that you've saved it.
  4. **Select your plan**: The "free" plan is enough for most folks. If you need more than the last 45 days worth of media (photos/videos/attachments), you can upgrade to the paid plan. 
  5. Then click **Back Up Now** to start the backup process.
  3. **Delete Signal**:  Finally, Delete the Signal app from your phone so the data is harder to access if your device is confiscated.

  <Alert type="warning">**Keep the Recovery Key safe.** If someone gets this key, they can read all of your Signal messages.</Alert>
</HowTo>

### Restoring Signal data

Once you are ready to restore your Signal data, you can do so by following these steps:

<HowTo title="How to restore Signal data">
  1. Reinstall the Signal app on your phone.
  2. Follow the steps to confirm your phone number.
  3. When prompted, select **Restore from Backup** to restore your Signal data.
  4. Enter your Recovery Key when prompted.
</HowTo>
