Enabling disappearing messages by default keeps you and your contacts safer if a phone is ever confiscated or breached. We don't know whose phone will be seized by law enforcement. Even if you believe you're talking about perfectly legal things, the government can find ways to twist your words.

WhatsApp's options are 24 hours, 7 days, or 90 days. 7 days is a good default to choose, and then you can manually set a shorter timeframe for more sensitive conversations.

<HowTo title="How to enable disappearing messages by default">
  <MenuPath>*iPhone:* WhatsApp > Tap **You** in the bottom bar > **Privacy** > **Default Message Timer** > pick your duration</MenuPath>

  <MenuPath>*Android:* WhatsApp > ⋮ > **Privacy** > **Default message timer** > pick your duration</MenuPath>
</HowTo>

<Alert type="info">
  The default timer only applies to **new chats you start**. If someone else starts a thread with you, the timer is not applied. To set it on an existing thread, open the chat > tap the name at the top > **Disappearing messages**.
</Alert>
