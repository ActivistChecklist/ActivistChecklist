Signal's encryption protects your messages as they travel across the internet. What it cannot protect against is a member of your group taking screenshots, downloading shared files to an unsecured location, or testifying about what was said. A group of 200 people means 200 phones, and you don't fully control what happens on any of them.

The habits below cost almost nothing and protect everyone in your group, not just you.

**Keep sensitive information out of large group chats.** If you need to share personal details (legal names, home addresses, phone numbers, workplace), send them in a direct message or a small, trusted group. The same goes for anything related to planned actions, evidence of participation in activities that could be perceived as crimes, or locations you frequent.

**Don't share real names or personal information about others.** If you need to refer to someone in a message, use their Signal display name or an @mention, not their legal name or details that would identify them outside the app. If you need to coordinate with a specific person, click their profile icon and message them directly.

**Never share your phone number in the group.** Other members can already reach you by clicking your profile. If someone is trying to connect with you outside Signal, share your **Signal link** or **username** instead of your number.

<HowTo title="How to find your Signal link or username">
  *On iPhone or Android (link):* Settings > tap the QR code icon > **Link** > **Copy Link**

  *On iPhone or Android (username):* Settings > tap your profile name > **@** (username) to set or copy
</HowTo>

**Avoid referencing people by name when you don't need to.** Use @mentions (which display however Signal renders the other person's name) or reply directly to a specific message rather than writing someone's name out.

<HowTo title="How to reply to a specific message">
  *On iPhone or Android:* Swipe right on the message, or long-press and select **Reply**

  *On desktop:* Hover over the message and click the reply arrow
</HowTo>

**Move sensitive conversations to smaller groups.** If you're planning something with a subset of people, make a separate group for just that conversation. A regional group of 200 is for coordination; the seven people planning a specific action should be somewhere else.

**Be skeptical of links.** Before clicking a link you didn't expect, copy it into a link checker. Signal's built-in link previews load destination content before you click. This is a known malware vector. We recommend disabling them entirely (see [Disable link previews](/checklist-items/signal-link-previews/)).

**Manually delete messages you regret.** Even without disappearing messages enabled, you can delete individual messages from your own device or from everyone in the thread.

<HowTo title="How to delete a message">
  Long-press the message > **Delete** > choose **Delete for Me** or **Delete for Everyone**
</HowTo>

<Alert type="info">
  "Delete for Everyone" removes the message from all recipients' devices going forward. It doesn't guarantee the recipient didn't already see or screenshot it, but it does limit future exposure.
</Alert>
