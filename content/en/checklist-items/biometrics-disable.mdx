<HowTo title="How to disable face/fingerprint unlock">
  <MenuPath>*iPhone:* **Settings** > **Face ID & Passcode** > Disable **Use Face ID for iPhone Unlock**</MenuPath>

  *(You can leave the rest enabled.)*

  <MenuPath>*Android:* **Settings** > **Lock Screen** (or **Security**) > **Biometrics and Security** > **Disable both Face Recognition and Fingerprint Unlock** for unlocking your phone</MenuPath>
</HowTo>

Caveat: Face and fingerprint unlock let you use stronger passwords without typing them all the time. If you have to choose, it's better to have a longer passcode than to turn off face/fingerprint unlock. If you leave biometrics on, make sure you know how to quickly turn them off if you're heading into a high-risk situation (see tips below).

<Alert type="success">
  **Fully powering off your phone is the best way to temporarily disable biometrics** because it also encrypts your data. The next unlock will require your passcode.
</Alert>

<Alert type="success">
  **iPhone locking tip:** If you leave face/fingerprint unlock on, you can force your passcode to be used for the next unlock by **pressing the side button 5 times**.
</Alert>

<Alert type="success">
  **iPhone camera tip:** You can take photos without unlocking your phone by **swiping left** on your home screen. (In case you get arrested while filming.)
</Alert>

<Alert type="success">
  **Android camera tip:** On many Android phones, you can launch the camera without unlocking by **pressing the side/power button twice**.
</Alert>
