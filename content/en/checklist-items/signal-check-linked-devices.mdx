Signal lets you link your account to desktop or tablet apps. That's useful for organizing work on a computer, but it also means someone who gets brief access to your unlocked phone can silently add a device and then read all your incoming messages from that point forward, without you ever knowing.

This is worth checking periodically, especially if you've been in situations where your phone was out of your hands: handed to someone else to show photos, left unattended at an action, or involved in any situation where law enforcement had brief access.

<HowTo title="How to check your linked devices">
  Signal > Settings > Linked Devices > **Review the list**

  If you see any device you don't recognize, tap it and select **Unlink**.

  If you don't have any desktop or tablet apps linked, the list should be empty. If it's not, that's a problem.
</HowTo>

If you find a device you don't recognize, treat it as a potential breach: change your Signal PIN, let your closest contacts know, and consider leaving and rejoining any groups you were active in.

<Alert type="success">
  Build this into your regular security hygiene. Check your linked devices after any action, protest, or situation where your phone was out of your control, even briefly.
</Alert>
