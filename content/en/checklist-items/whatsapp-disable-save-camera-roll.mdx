Photos and videos you receive on WhatsApp can end up in more places than you think. By default, WhatsApp may save them straight to your camera roll, and iCloud or Google Photos will then back those up to the cloud automatically.

That means sensitive images (protest footage, photos of organizers, anything that could expose your community) and their metadata (such as the location and time the photo was taken) can sit in a Google or Apple account where law enforcement can subpoena them. Deleting every copy later becomes much harder.

<HowTo title="How to disable auto-save for all chats">
  <MenuPath>*iPhone:* WhatsApp > **Settings** > **Chats** > disable **Save to Camera Roll**</MenuPath>

  <MenuPath>*Android:* WhatsApp > **Settings** > **Chats** > disable **Media Visibility**</MenuPath>
</HowTo>

<HowTo title="How to disable auto-save for a specific chat or group">
  <MenuPath>*iPhone:* Open the chat > tap the chat name at the top > **Save to Camera Roll** > choose **Default**, **Always**, or **Never**</MenuPath>

  <MenuPath>*Android:* Open the chat > tap the chat name at the top > **Media visibility** > choose **Default**, **Yes**, or **No**</MenuPath>
</HowTo>
