Cada clic, mensaje y señal de ubicación crea un rastro digital que puede usarse en contra de activistas y organizadores. Las fuerzas del orden exigen regularmente datos a las empresas tecnológicas para identificar y vigilar a las personas que trabajan por el cambio social.

**Esta guía te ayuda a minimizar tu rastro digital.** Estos pasos no te harán invisible, pero dificultarán considerablemente que las autoridades puedan:

* Rastrear tu ubicación y tus patrones de movimiento
* Monitorear tus comunicaciones y debates políticos
* Mapear tus relaciones y redes
* Crear perfiles de tus actividades y asociaciones

<Alert type="info">
  **Más fácil en grupo.** La mayoría de la gente tiene la intención de hacer estos pasos y nunca los hace. Considera trabajar esta lista con amigues en una [fiesta de seguridad](/party/): 90 minutos, unos snacks, y progreso real que ayuda a hacer tu comunidad más segura.
</Alert>

<FAQ>
  <FAQItem question="How long does the essentials checklist actually take?" answer="The first four items take about 30 minutes and give you the biggest privacy gains. The full baseline section is usually 45 to 60 minutes. The enhanced section adds another hour or two depending on which items you do. Most people who try to do it all alone never finish, which is why we suggest running this with friends at a security party." />
  <FAQItem question="Do I need to create new accounts to do all of this?" answer="Just one: a Proton Mail account for end-to-end encrypted email and docs. Everything else uses apps and settings on the phone you already have. You do not need new social media accounts, a new phone number, or special hardware." />
  <FAQItem question="Is iPhone or Android better for activists?" answer="iPhone is the safer default for most people. Apple ships security updates faster and longer, and iOS has stronger sandboxing between apps. Advanced users can get more privacy and control from a Google Pixel running GrapheneOS, but it takes time to set up and to live with the app limitations." />
  <FAQItem question="What if I cannot do all of this right now?" answer="Do the first section. The five items there cover the biggest risks: using Signal for messages, turning off location for non-essential apps, using a privacy-focused browser and search engine, and setting a strong passcode. You will get most of the protection from doing just those, and you can come back for the rest." />
  <FAQItem question="Will any of this stop the FBI or NSA if I am specifically targeted?" answer="No, and any guide that claims otherwise is misleading you. A well-resourced state actor that decides to surveil one specific person has tools most defenses cannot stop. What this checklist does is reduce mass surveillance, make warrant returns less useful, slow down forensic searches of seized phones, and protect the network of people connected to you." />
</FAQ>
