Meta recently rolled out a [feature](https://redact.dev/blog/meta-camera-roll-ai-sharing-suggestions-2025) in the Facebook app where all images in your photo library are sent to their servers and processed. This is a significant violation of privacy and could present legal risk to activists.

Meta (the parent company that also owns WhatsApp) has claimed that this kind of feature doesn't exist for WhatsApp. Even so, it's worth disabling broad photo access for WhatsApp, since the company rolled out the exact feature on Facebook.

<HowTo title="How to give WhatsApp limited access to your photo library">
  Limited access adds one or two steps when you want to share a new photo, but it's simple.

  <MenuPath>*iPhone:* **Settings** app > **Apps** > **WhatsApp** > **Photos** > select **Limited Access**</MenuPath>

  <MenuPath>*Android:* **Settings** app > **Apps** > **WhatsApp** > **Permissions** > **Photos and videos** > select **Select photos and videos every time** (or **Ask every time**, depending on your device)</MenuPath>
</HowTo>
