If you're running an organizing group, the settings on the group itself matter as much as your personal settings.

Group chat privacy is only as strong as the least secure member. If one person is an infiltrator or has a compromised device, everything in the chat can be exposed. Support your group to follow norms together.

<HowTo title="How to lock down your group settings">
  **Recommended group setup:**

  <InlineChecklist>
    * **Enable disappearing messages on the group from the start.** Pick the shortest timeframe the group can tolerate.
    * **Enable [Advanced Chat Privacy](/whatsapp/#whatsapp-advanced-chat-privacy)** on the group. This disables chat export, prevents media from auto-downloading to everyone's camera roll, and prevents Meta AI from reading the chat.
    * **Make the group invite-only.** <MenuPath inline>Open the group thread > tap the group name > **Group Permissions** > restrict who can edit info, add members, and send messages</MenuPath>.
    * **Agree on group norms up front:** no cloud backups, no discussion of high-risk actions on WhatsApp, don't add new people without approval. Make sure all new members agree to these norms, and remind existing members periodically.
    * **Design a vetting practice that fits your needs and risk tolerance.** Larger groups are easier to infiltrate.
  </InlineChecklist>
</HowTo>
