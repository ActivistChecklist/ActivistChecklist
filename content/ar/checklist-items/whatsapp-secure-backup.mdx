The safest option is to leave backups off entirely (see [Disable WhatsApp chat backups](/whatsapp/#whatsapp-disable-backups)). Only enable backups if you actually need them, for example if you're transferring devices or crossing a border and need to temporarily delete WhatsApp.

If you do need backups, turn on WhatsApp's built-in end-to-end encrypted backup. Without this, Google or Apple can be compelled to hand your entire chat history over to law enforcement.

<HowTo title="How to enable encrypted backups">
  <MenuPath>*iPhone:* WhatsApp > Tap **You** in the bottom bar > **Chats** > **Chat Backup** > **End-to-end encrypted backup** > set a passkey or password</MenuPath>

  <MenuPath>*Android:* WhatsApp > ⋮ > **Chats** > **Chat Backup** > **End-to-end encrypted backup** > set a passkey or password</MenuPath>

  **Save the password in a [password manager](/essentials/#password-manager).** WhatsApp cannot recover it for you.
</HowTo>

<Alert type="info">
  **iPhone only:** For extra protection on top of WhatsApp's encrypted backup, you can also turn on Apple's **Advanced Data Protection for iCloud**. This is a separate, iCloud-wide setting that end-to-end encrypts most of what's in your iCloud account.
</Alert>
